Task 2: Phishing Email Detection & Awareness

1. Introduction:
This report analyzes email samples to detect phishing attempts and improve awareness.

2. Objective:
To identify phishing emails and educate users about prevention.

3. Tools Used:
- Manual Analysis
- Browser inspection

4. Email Analysis:

Email 1:
Subject: Urgent! Your bank account will be suspended
Sender Email: support@secure-bank-login.xyz (Suspicious)

- Contains urgent message
- Uses fake link (http://secure-bank-login.xyz)
- Generic greeting
→ Classified as PHISHING

Email 2:
Subject: Monthly Bank Statement
Sender Email: official@bank.com (Legitimate)

- Legitimate message
- No suspicious links
→ Classified as SAFE

5. Phishing Indicators:
- Urgent language
- Suspicious links
- Generic greetings
- Threatening tone

6. Prevention Tips:
- Do not click unknown links
- Check sender email carefully
- Verify URLs before entering data
- Use official apps/websites

7. Conclusion:
Phishing emails can trick users into revealing sensitive information. Awareness is key to prevention.
